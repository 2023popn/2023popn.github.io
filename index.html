Field Notes

Why I care about grams and miles

Time outside is what keeps the "why" in view. Hiking and climbing aren't research for my engineering — they're the reason I want the work to matter. Clean air on a ridgeline and a quiet trail are easy to take for granted until you think about what keeps them that way.

That's the throughline to electrification for me. Transportation is one of the largest sources of emissions, and every gram and every watt-hour I can make a battery pack do more efficiently is a small argument for cleaner air and fewer places we have to worry about protecting.

It's why the sustainability-minded companies on my list — EV manufacturers, clean energy, and materials innovators — feel like a continuation of the same values, not a separate track from my coursework.
